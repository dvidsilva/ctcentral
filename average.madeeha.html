What are these headsets doing?

The Muse headset measures brain signals: much like a heart rate monitor measures your heartbeat. The neurons in our brain communicate via electronic pulses sent from one neuron to the other. When a large number of neurons fire at the same rate, they create an electronic waves that we can measure from outside the head.

What kinds of waves are we measuring?

  • Delta Waves are the slowest waves (0-4 Hz) and are present mostly during sleep.
  • Theta Waves (4-7 Hz) are occur during sleep or very deep relaxation.
  • Alpha Waves (7-13 Hz) are given off when one is calm and relaxed.
  • Beta (13-30 Hz) are emitted when we are consciously alert, or when we feel tense, agitated, or afraid.
  • Gamma are the fastest-moving waves (30-70 Hz) and are associated with cognitive processing and sudden insight.
